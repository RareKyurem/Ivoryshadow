A lot of schools have recently held polls on voting day, but this year they may refrain from doing so because of the potential tension.

In past years, schools have traditionally hosted polling stations because of their large facilities and parking spaces.

The contrasting presidential candidates might lead to some tension between voters and result in a fight, something not needed on a school campus.

Many schools in the northeast have canceled classes or moved buildings to protect students.

The fears come from the apparent violence in this year's voting season. This past year, a voting office has been bombed, and protesters have threatened violence.

A secondary reason is that school closing could potentially increase voter turnout, which could be important because this election could be close.

Proponents argue that students miss out on already needed learning time forces them to makeup assignments.

One day wouldn't really be that much of an issue. Just have students self-study while they get school off.

China Stops Harvesting Organs from Inmates

Sunday, October 16, 2:55 P.M.

A conference in Beijing dealt with China's inmate organ harvesting.

Doctors from around the world met to discuss how China's ban on inmate harvesting has affected China so far. China has been reporting a shortage of organ donors, but doubts persist about the validity of the statements.

To emphasize the validity of these statements and its importance, China hosted the conference in the Great Hall of the People, which is typically only used by foreign leaders.

The past vice president of health also divulged that 90% of organs came from executed inmates.

Family members of inmates do have the opportunity to veto any transplants they disapprove of.

A Canadian patient has also received an organ from an inmate earlier this year, and the surgeon was suspended from any further transplants.
